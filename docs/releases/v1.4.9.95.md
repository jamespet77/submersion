# 🎉 Submersion v1.4.9.95 Release Notes

This release is a maintenance update that follows on from the surface-GPS and automatic dive-site matching work of the previous two versions. Its most important change makes sure the Shearwater exit-GPS fix is actually present in the builds you download; the rest are smaller corrections to the Statistics charts, the Courses feature, and the site-match workflow.

#### Bug fixes

#### Shearwater surface GPS now correct

Dives downloaded from a Shearwater computer with Swift GPS were recording the same coordinates for water entry and exit, which left both the surface-GPS drift readout and the new automatic site-matching unable to tell the two points apart. Distinguishing the exit position requires a small patch to the underlying libdivecomputer library, and while that patch was applied in local development builds, the automated builds that users actually download were compiled against the unpatched library and returned the entry coordinate for both points. The patch is now vendored into the libdivecomputer library itself, so every build — local or downloaded — captures separate entry and exit positions. Shearwater Swift GPS dives downloaded with this version will record distinct entry/exit coordinates, and surface drift is calculated from real movement again. Dives downloaded under earlier builds keep the coordinates they were saved with; re-downloading them from the computer with this version picks up the corrected positions.

#### Statistics: "Time at Depth Ranges" chart respects your depth unit

In the Statistics section, the Time at Depth Ranges chart always labelled its axis in metres and grouped dives by metric depth boundaries, even for divers who log in feet. The chart now converts its depth ranges to your configured depth unit and labels the axis to match, so the buckets are consistent with the rest of the app.

#### Courses: dates and duration

Two issues in the Courses feature are fixed. Dates shown on course cards, in the course picker, on the detail page, and in the edit form ignored your date-format preference and used a fixed format; they now follow the same date-format setting as the rest of the app. Separately, course duration was counted exclusively — a course running 27–29 May was reported as 2 days instead of 3, and could be skewed further by the time of day a course was created. Duration is now counted inclusively by calendar day, so both the first and last day are included.

#### Dive-site matching: list refreshes after applying matches

After matching dives to sites — whether automatically following a download or through the "Match Dives to Sites" action — the dive list kept showing "unknown site" for the newly linked dives until the app was restarted. The list now refreshes as soon as matches are applied, so linked site names appear immediately.
